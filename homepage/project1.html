shoe cabinet

The idea behind this project was to give it an old-fashoned Japanase-style look. The original inspiration were tradtional kumiko wooden panels. Our cabinet was made out of Polish oak. The main body was made out of four glued-up oak panels. Then, they were conencted on the edges via traditional finger joints. We left them exposed because they complement the overall construction in our view. Please note that the sliding doors and their frames are all made of wood and jointed only with the use of glue. The cabinet was finished with the use of hot linseed oil and natural wax in order to secure the beautiful honey-like color.
